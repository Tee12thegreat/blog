Fundamentals of Sustainable Gardening

Sustainable gardening is an approach that focuses on creating an ecologically balanced garden that requires minimal external inputs and creates minimal waste.

Key Principles

  • Work with nature rather than against it
  • Conserve water and other resources
  • Build healthy soil naturally
  • Promote biodiversity
  • Reduce waste through recycling and composting

Getting Started

Begin by assessing your space, sunlight, and soil conditions. Choose native plants that are adapted to your local climate and require less maintenance.

Water Conservation Techniques

Efficient water use is crucial for sustainable gardening. Here are several methods to reduce water consumption:

Rainwater Harvesting

Collecting rainwater in barrels or cisterns can provide a free water source for your garden while reducing runoff.

Drip Irrigation

This targeted watering system delivers water directly to plant roots, minimizing evaporation and runoff.
